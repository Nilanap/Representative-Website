Why Reaching Out Matters

Your vote on a bill is just the start—contacting your representatives is the most important step to ensure your voice shapes the laws that affect us all. By sharing your stance, like your vote on a bill through our platform, you directly influence decisions in Congress. Representatives rely on input from constituents to understand what matters to you. Make your perspective count!

Example Email to Send

Here’s a sample email you can use or adapt when contacting your representative through Democracy.io:

To: Representative Jon Ossoff
Subject: Opposition to the McCain Library Bill

Dear Representative Ossoff,

As a resident of Georgia, I am writing to express my disapproval of the proposed McCain Library bill. While I respect the intent to honor a public figure, I am concerned that this project will contribute to our national debt, which already burdens future generations. I believe we should prioritize fiscal responsibility and focus on initiatives that directly address urgent needs in our state, like infrastructure or education.

My name is Jane Doe, a small business owner and parent in Atlanta. I’ve lived in Georgia my entire life and care deeply about our community’s future. I urge you to vote against this bill and consider alternatives that align with our state’s long-term financial health.

Thank you for your time and service.

Sincerely,
Jane Doe
